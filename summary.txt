Datathon 2026 — Strategy Exploration Summary
==============================================

PROBLEM
-------
Per-session scalar target_position. Score = Sharpe(pnl)·16 where
pnl_i = position_i · (close_99/close_49 − 1). 1,000 train sessions,
10,000 public + 10,000 private test sessions. 50 seen OHLC bars +
~10 headlines (bar_ix ≤ 49) per session. FinBERT sentiment precomputed.

Target stats on train: mean r ≈ +0.00356, std ≈ 0.02012, P(r>0) ≈ 57.7%.

FIRST-PRINCIPLES FRAMEWORK
--------------------------
Sharpe-optimal Kelly-style position:
    p_i ∝ (μ_i + γ) / σ²_i
Decomposed into:
  - Mean head:     μ̂ = Ridge(standardized features)
  - Variance head: σ̂² = exp(Ridge on log((r-r̄)²)), floored at median
  - Position rule: winsorize, normalize by median(|raw|), shrink toward
                   p_const=1 with weight λ
    p = (1-λ)·1 + λ·(winsorized raw / median)

ROOT CAUSES OF PRIOR OVERFIT (designed against)
-----------------------------------------------
1. Forward leakage: fwd_log_vol_k5, ThisCompany/Sector columns used
   future or train-wide aggregates. REMOVED — rebuilt features.py from
   scratch, all features strictly per-session on bars 0..49.
2. Parameter/sample ratio: 150+ one-hot columns on N=1000. REMOVED —
   capped at 22 base features.
3. Post-processing tuned on same folds as model. FIX: nested CV
   (10-fold outer × 5-fold inner), bootstrap CI (2000 resamples).
4. No shrinkage to known prior (constant-long Sharpe ≈ 2.77 on train,
   2.21 on LB). FIX: λ-shrinkage + 1.5·SE acceptance gate.

KEY EMPIRICAL FINDINGS
----------------------
- All directional signal lives in bars 40-49: corr(sent_last10, r)
  = +0.059, sentiment in earlier bins ~0.
- 80 distinct fictional companies shared across train/test.
- 632 distinct headline templates; top 14 cover ~31% of all headlines.
- Primary-company filter (entity = first 2 words of headline) actively
  HURTS signal: corr(primary_sent_last10, r) = +0.019 << +0.059.
  Most session-relevant signal is NOT company-specific — we can't
  reliably identify which of the 80 companies is each session's target.

FAILED APPROACHES & WHY
-----------------------
1. Template impact (Bayesian-shrunk target encoding on 632 templates):
   pushed p_std to 0.68 and dropped corr(μ̂,r) from 0.08 to 0.03. Too
   little data per template (median ~15 sessions) → noise dominates.
2. Interaction features (sent_last10 × rv, neg × drawdown, etc.): all
   hurt CV by ~0.1 Sharpe. No interactions survive regularization.
3. Primary-company relevance features: hurt because the target stock
   identity isn't reliably derivable from headline-entity frequency.
4. CatBoost mean head (depth 2–3, 300–400 iterations, l2=10–20):
   consistently 0.10-0.15 below Ridge. Tree-based overfitting shows
   up at N=1000 even with strong regularization.
5. Ensembles (Ridge+CatBoost, Ridge+Ridge at different α): within
   CV noise of v1. No diversification benefit because all models
   draw signal from the same thin ~bar-40-49 feature band.
6. Higher-λ shrinkage to constant-long (λ=0.5, 0.75): CV barely
   improves (+0.02), but LB gets WORSE — CV signal reversed from LB.

WHY THE CV→LB GAP
-----------------
v1: CV 2.793 → LB 2.407 (gap 0.39)
constant_long: CV 2.765 → LB 2.212 (gap 0.55)
Ridge ACTUALLY has a smaller gap than constant-long, suggesting the
model is capturing real signal. But the gap magnitude (~0.5·SE)
indicates genuine distribution shift between train (1k sessions)
and test (10k public + 10k private). Cannot be closed by CV tuning.

CONCLUSION
----------
Ship v1 (sharpe_v1.csv, LB 2.407). All attempts to beat it in CV
have been within ±1·SE = noise. The 0.45-Sharpe gap to leader (2.86)
is ~1·SE — either they've found a structurally different signal
(candidate: robust session-target-stock identification from bar
microstructure, which we couldn't get) or public LB luck. Further
CV iteration is selection bias, not progress.
